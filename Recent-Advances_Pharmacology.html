Pain Management

Suzetrigine

Journavx® FDA approved · Jan 2025

Class: Selective peripheral NaV1.8 voltage-gated sodium-channel blocker (non-opioid analgesic)

Mechanism of Action

Selectively blocks the NaV1.8 voltage-gated sodium channel expressed almost exclusively on peripheral nociceptive (pain-sensing) neurons. By stabilising the channel in a non-conducting state it stops the propagation of pain action potentials before they reach the CNS. It does not cross the blood–brain barrier — so no euphoria, dependence, sedation, or respiratory depression.

Mechanism Visual

Why It Matters

The first genuinely new oral analgesic mechanism in ~25 years and the first non-opioid approved specifically for moderate-to-severe acute pain. Offers opioid-level targeting of pain without addiction potential — a direct answer to the opioid-crisis unmet need.

Exam Relevance / High-Yield

  • Target: NaV1.8 (contrast with local anaesthetics = non-selective NaV block; carbamazepine/phenytoin = NaV in CNS).
  • Selectivity: >30,000-fold over other NaV subtypes → peripheral action, spares CNS/cardiac channels.
  • Not a controlled substance — no tolerance/dependence; key MCQ distractor vs opioids.
  • Metabolised by CYP3A4 → caution with strong 3A4 inhibitors/inducers.
Clinical Pearl

Think of suzetrigine as a "peripheral, oral local-anaesthetic-like" analgesic that is subtype-selective — it silences the nociceptor's own sodium channel rather than dampening the whole nervous system.

NEET PG Tip

Mnemonic — "1.8 = Ate the pain, not the brain." NaV1.8 is peripheral-nociceptor specific; no BBB crossing = no opioid-type CNS effects.

Cross-links: Special Systems (Pain) General Pharmacology

Reference: Vertex — FDA approval of JOURNAVX (suzetrigine)

Quick recall — Pain

Which ion channel does suzetrigine selectively inhibit, and why does that avoid CNS side effects?
NaV1.8, a voltage-gated sodium channel expressed on peripheral nociceptors. Suzetrigine does not cross the blood–brain barrier, so it produces analgesia without sedation, euphoria, dependence, or respiratory depression.
Suzetrigine is the first non-opioid analgesic of a new mechanistic class in approximately how many years?
About 25 years — it is the first novel-mechanism oral analgesic class for acute pain in over two decades.
Name one clinically important drug-interaction consideration with suzetrigine.
It is a CYP3A4 substrate — strong 3A4 inhibitors raise its levels and strong inducers lower efficacy.

Metabolic / Endocrine

Tirzepatide

Mounjaro® / Zepbound® Approved (T2DM 2022 · obesity 2023)

Class: Dual GIP + GLP-1 receptor agonist ("twincretin")

Mechanism of Action

A single peptide that activates both the GIP and GLP-1 incretin receptors → enhanced glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppressed glucagon, delayed gastric emptying, and central appetite suppression. The added GIP agonism is thought to amplify weight loss and insulin sensitivity beyond GLP-1 alone.

Why It Matters

Produces the greatest weight loss and HbA1c reduction of any injectable to date, redefining expectations for both T2DM and obesity pharmacotherapy.

Exam Relevance / High-Yield

  • "Twincretin" — dual GIP/GLP-1 vs semaglutide (GLP-1 only) and retatrutide (triple).
  • Weekly SC injection; GI side effects (nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea) are dose-limiting.
  • Shared class warnings: medullary thyroid carcinoma / MEN-2 contraindication, pancreatitis, gallbladder disease.
NEET PG Tip

Count the receptors: Semaglutide = 1 (GLP-1) · Tirzepatide = 2 (GIP+GLP-1) · Retatrutide = 3 (+glucagon).

Cross-links: Endocrine Pharmacology

Reference: Nature Medicine — The expanding landscape of GLP-1 medicines

Orforglipron

Late-stage / filing 2025–26

Class: Oral non-peptide (small-molecule) GLP-1 receptor agonist

Mechanism of Action

A small molecule that activates the GLP-1 receptor — unlike peptide GLP-1 drugs it is orally bioavailable, needs no dietary restriction and no refrigeration.

Why It Matters

Could bring GLP-1 efficacy in a cheap, scalable daily pill — a major access/adherence advance over injectables and over peptide oral semaglutide (which needs strict fasting dosing).

Exam Relevance / High-Yield

  • Contrast with oral semaglutide (a peptide + SNAC absorption enhancer, strict empty-stomach dosing).
  • "Non-peptide" = not degraded like a peptide → simpler pharmacokinetics, no cold chain.
Cross-links: Endocrine Pharmacology

Reference: IQVIA — Outlook for Obesity in 2026

Retatrutide

Phase 3 (investigational)

Class: Triple GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon receptor agonist

Mechanism of Action

Adds glucagon-receptor agonism to the incretin effect. Glucagon agonism raises energy expenditure and promotes hepatic fat oxidation, complementing appetite suppression for even greater weight loss.

Why It Matters

Trial weight-loss magnitudes approach those of bariatric surgery, and the glucagon arm is being explored for MASLD/NAFLD (fatty liver).

Clinical Pearl

Glucagon agonism seems paradoxical in diabetes, but the strong incretin (insulinotropic) component offsets it while the added energy expenditure drives fat loss.

Cross-links: Endocrine Pharmacology

Reference: Nature Medicine — GLP-1 medicines landscape

Quick recall — Metabolic

What distinguishes tirzepatide from semaglutide mechanistically?
Tirzepatide is a dual GIP + GLP-1 receptor agonist ("twincretin"); semaglutide is a GLP-1-only agonist.
Which three receptors does retatrutide target?
GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors — a "triple agonist."
Why is orforglipron a notable formulation advance over injectable GLP-1s?
It is an oral, non-peptide small molecule — no injection, no refrigeration, and no strict fasting requirement.

Antimicrobials & Infectious Diseases

Gepotidacin

Blujepa® FDA approved · 2025

Class: First-in-class triazaacenaphthylene bacterial topoisomerase inhibitor

Mechanism of Action

Inhibits bacterial DNA replication by a novel dual-target mechanism — it blocks both topoisomerase II (DNA gyrase, GyrA) and topoisomerase IV (ParC) at a site distinct from fluoroquinolones. The dual binding raises the barrier to resistance.

Why It Matters

First new oral antibiotic class for uncomplicated UTI in decades; retains activity against many fluoroquinolone- and multidrug-resistant strains, including N. gonorrhoeae.

Exam Relevance / High-Yield

  • Same enzymes as fluoroquinolones but different binding site → little cross-resistance (key MCQ point).
  • Indication: uncomplicated UTI (E. coli, S. saprophyticus); also studied for gonorrhoea.
NEET PG Tip

Both gepotidacin and fluoroquinolones hit gyrase/topo IV — but the novel site is why resistant organisms stay susceptible. Compare with zoliflodacin below.

Cross-links: Infectious Disease Chemotherapy

Reference: FDA — Novel Drug Approvals 2025

Zoliflodacin

Filed / Phase 3 complete

Class: First-in-class spiropyrimidinetrione (bacterial type II topoisomerase inhibitor)

Mechanism of Action

Traps the bacterial type II topoisomerase, blocking DNA re-ligation — again a novel binding site distinct from fluoroquinolones, so no cross-resistance.

Why It Matters

A single oral dose cure for uncomplicated gonorrhoea, including multidrug-resistant N. gonorrhoeae — a WHO priority pathogen where treatment options are running out.

Clinical Pearl

Both new antibiotics here target type II topoisomerases at novel sites — the field's answer to eroding fluoroquinolone and cephalosporin efficacy in gonorrhoea.

Cross-links: Infectious Disease Chemotherapy

Reference: A year in pharmacology: FDA new drugs 2025 (Springer)

Quick recall — Antimicrobials

Why do gepotidacin and zoliflodacin retain activity against fluoroquinolone-resistant organisms?
They inhibit the same type II topoisomerase enzymes but bind at a novel site distinct from fluoroquinolones, so existing quinolone-resistance mutations do not confer cross-resistance.
What is the flagship indication for gepotidacin (Blujepa)?
Uncomplicated urinary tract infection (uUTI); it is also being developed for gonorrhoea.
Zoliflodacin offers what practical dosing advantage for gonorrhoea?
A single oral dose cure, effective against multidrug-resistant N. gonorrhoeae.

Oncology & Targeted Therapies

Revumenib

Revuforj® FDA approved · 2024–25

Class: First-in-class oral menin inhibitor

Mechanism of Action

Disrupts the menin–KMT2A (MLL) protein–protein interaction that drives aberrant leukaemic gene transcription, forcing malignant blasts to differentiate. Approved for relapsed/refractory acute leukaemia with KMT2A rearrangement.

Why It Matters

A precision epigenetic target for a genetically defined, hard-to-treat leukaemia — exemplifies the shift from cytotoxics to molecularly targeted therapy.

NEET PG Tip

Keyword pairing: menin inhibitor → KMT2A/MLL-rearranged (and NPM1-mutated) acute leukaemia. Mechanism = induces differentiation.

ADCs & Radioligand Therapy

Rapidly expanding class

Class: Targeted cytotoxic-delivery platforms (antibody–drug conjugates & radioligands)

Mechanism of Action

ADCs = a monoclonal antibody (targeting a tumour antigen, e.g. HER2, TROP2) linked to a potent cytotoxic "payload" — the antibody delivers the toxin selectively to cancer cells, sparing normal tissue. Radioligand therapy couples a tumour-targeting ligand (e.g. PSMA) to a radioisotope (e.g. lutetium-177) that irradiates the cell from within.

Mechanism Visual

Why It Matters

Widen the therapeutic window — more tumour kill with less systemic toxicity. Along with CAR-T and bispecific antibodies, they are the fastest-growing segment of oncology approvals.

Exam Relevance / High-Yield

  • ADC parts: antibody + linker + payload (know all three).
  • Classic examples: trastuzumab emtansine/deruxtecan (HER2), sacituzumab govitecan (TROP2).
  • Radioligand: Lu-177 PSMA (prostate), Lu-177 DOTATATE (NET).
  • CAR-T = autologous T-cells engineered with a chimeric antigen receptor (e.g. anti-CD19).
Cross-links: Neoplastic Disease Pharmacology Immunity & Haematopoiesis

Reference: HCPLive — Novel drug approvals, 2026

Quick recall — Oncology

Name the three structural components of an antibody–drug conjugate.
A monoclonal antibody (targeting), a linker, and a cytotoxic payload.
Revumenib targets which protein–protein interaction, and in which leukaemia subtype?
The menin–KMT2A (MLL) interaction, in KMT2A-rearranged (and NPM1-mutated) acute leukaemia; it induces blast differentiation.
Which radioisotope-ligand pairing is used for metastatic prostate cancer?
Lutetium-177 linked to a PSMA ligand (Lu-177 PSMA radioligand therapy).

Neurology & Psychiatry

Xanomeline–Trospium

Cobenfy® FDA approved · Sep 2024

Class: M1/M4 muscarinic receptor agonist (xanomeline) + peripheral muscarinic antagonist (trospium)

Mechanism of Action

Xanomeline preferentially activates central M1/M4 muscarinic receptors, modulating dopamine indirectly — the first antipsychotic that works without directly blocking D2 receptors. Trospium is a peripherally restricted muscarinic antagonist added purely to limit xanomeline's peripheral cholinergic side effects (nausea, sweating, GI).

Why It Matters

First novel antipsychotic mechanism in decades. Because it spares D2 blockade, it avoids classic extrapyramidal symptoms, hyperprolactinaemia, and tardive dyskinesia.

Exam Relevance / High-Yield

  • Non-dopaminergic antipsychotic — the single highest-yield contrast vs all typical/atypical agents.
  • Trospium's job = block peripheral muscarinic effects (it does not cross the BBB).
  • No EPS, no prolactin elevation, no significant weight-gain/metabolic D2-type profile.
NEET PG Tip

"Muscarinic, not dopaminergic." Xanomeline = M1/M4 agonist for schizophrenia; trospium = peripheral M-blocker bodyguard. No D2 block → no EPS/prolactin.

Reference: FDA — Novel Drug Approvals

Lecanemab & Donanemab

Leqembi® / Kisunla® Approved (2023 & 2024)

Class: Anti-amyloid-β monoclonal antibodies (disease-modifying, early Alzheimer's)

Mechanism of Action

Monoclonal antibodies that bind and clear amyloid-β — lecanemab targets soluble protofibrils; donanemab targets deposited plaque (pyroglutamate Aβ). Clearing amyloid modestly slows cognitive decline in early disease.

Why It Matters

First therapies to modify the disease course of Alzheimer's rather than just treat symptoms (contrast with cholinesterase inhibitors and memantine).

Exam Relevance / High-Yield

  • Key adverse effect: ARIA (Amyloid-Related Imaging Abnormalities — oedema/microhaemorrhage); higher risk in ApoE ε4 homozygotes → MRI monitoring.
  • Disease-modifying vs symptomatic (donepezil/rivastigmine/galantamine, memantine).
  • Note negative result: semaglutide did not slow Alzheimer's in the EVOKE trial.
Clinical Pearl

Any anti-amyloid mAb in an ApoE ε4 carrier = watch for ARIA on surveillance MRI; it is the defining class toxicity.

Cross-links: Special Systems (CNS) Neuropharmacology

Reference: BrightFocus — Alzheimer's treatment updates

Quick recall — Neuro/Psych

Why does Cobenfy (xanomeline–trospium) avoid extrapyramidal symptoms?
Xanomeline is an M1/M4 muscarinic agonist and does not directly block D2 receptors, so it avoids EPS, hyperprolactinaemia, and tardive dyskinesia.
What is the role of trospium in the combination?
A peripherally restricted muscarinic antagonist that blunts xanomeline's peripheral cholinergic side effects without entering the CNS.
What is the hallmark adverse effect of anti-amyloid monoclonal antibodies?
ARIA (Amyloid-Related Imaging Abnormalities — oedema and microhaemorrhage), especially in ApoE ε4 carriers.

Cross-cutting Trends

AI in Drug Discovery

Trend

Machine-learning models now accelerate target identification, molecule design, and in-silico toxicity prediction, shortening early discovery timelines and reducing failed candidates before human trials.

NEET PG Tip

Understand the concept: AI shifts effort "left" into discovery/preclinical — it doesn't replace clinical trials for safety/efficacy.

Personalised / Precision Medicine

Trend

Pharmacogenomics tailors drug and dose to the patient's genotype. High-yield examples: CYP2C19 (clopidogrel), TPMT/NUDT15 (thiopurines), HLA-B*15:02 (carbamazepine SJS), DPYD (5-FU/capecitabine).

Clinical Pearl

These genotype–drug pairs are perennial MCQ favourites — memorise the enzyme/allele and the drug it flags.

Cross-links: General Pharmacology

Resistance Patterns (AMR)

Trend

Rising multidrug resistance (ESBL, carbapenem-resistant Enterobacterales, MDR gonorrhoea) is driving new-class antibiotics (see gepotidacin, zoliflodacin) and antimicrobial stewardship as core practice.

Cross-links: Infectious Disease Chemotherapy

Regulatory & Modality Shifts

Trend

More than half of new approvals now use expedited pathways (fast-track/breakthrough/accelerated). Biologics, mRNA, gene & cell therapies, and radiopharmaceuticals make up a growing share, while biosimilars expand access as originator biologics lose exclusivity.

NEET PG Tip

Know the modality ladder: small molecule → biologic/mAb → ADC → cell/gene therapy → radioligand — increasing target precision.

Quick recall — Trends

Which gene predicts carbamazepine-induced Stevens–Johnson syndrome (esp. in Asian populations)?
HLA-B*15:02 — test before starting carbamazepine in at-risk populations.
Give one high-yield pharmacogenomic pair for thiopurine dosing.
TPMT (and NUDT15) — deficiency → severe myelosuppression with azathioprine/6-MP.
What is the main clinical driver behind new-class antibiotics like gepotidacin?
Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) — eroding efficacy of fluoroquinolones/cephalosporins demands novel mechanisms.
